Metasequoia can extrude faces, but it removes original faces. So you have to first select faces you want to extrude, and then duplicate that. You can duplicate entire object with `Clone`, or copy&paste.

This will create new object, so select all the faces and press `Invert`.

{% include discord-gallery.html id="gallery1" %}

Then you can move on to extruding faces.

1. Press E or click Extrude
2. select faces by Shift+click (outline turns cyan)

After that, click&drag horizontally to adjust width.

{% include discord-gallery.html id="gallery2" %}

{% include videoPlayer.html url="Screen_Recording_2025-09-05_at_4.17.30-d27f0cc61692d81d.mp4" %}

After extruding, you have to merge vertices.

First, merge the two objects by drag&drop.

Then, change to `Modeling (Icon)`. Without changing, `Join closed vertices` won't appear.

After that, click `Join closed vertices` and proceed. In my case, 8 vertices have been merged.
